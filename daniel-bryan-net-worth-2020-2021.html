    Early Life

    Daniel Bryan was born on May 22, 1981, in Aberdeen, Washington, as a Bryan Lloyd Danielson. His father was a logger, and his mother worked as a therapist. Daniel is American by nationality. He completed his graduation from Aberdeen Weatherwax High School. During school days, he was the good football player. After graduation, he decided to make a career in Wrestling and joined Dean Malenko Wrestling School, but eventually, that got closed.

    Personal Life

    Later he joined Texas Wrestling Academy, where he was trained by professional wrestlers Shawn Michael and Rudy Gonzalez. Daniel is married to an attractive professional wrestler named ‘Brie Bella’ in 2014, and in May 2017, they welcome their first child named ‘Birdie Joe Danielson.’ After completing sixteen years of the career in Wrestling, he chose to take retirement in February 2016 due to some serious neck injury.

    Age, Height, and Weight

    Being born on 22 May 1981, Daniel Bryan is 43 years old as of today’s date 20th July 2024. His height is 1.78 m tall, and his weight is 95 kg.

    Career

    Daniel’s career began officially when he signed a contract with the WWE and assigned for the development program of the Memphis Championship wrestling in 2000. Unfortunately, WWE severed its tie with Memphis Championship Wrestling in 2001, and Daniel got released from the contract. Later he joined Texas Wrestling Alliance (TWA) from where he won his very first Championship as a Light Heavyweight Championship. Due to his great performance in the ring, he honored with the ring name ‘American Dragon.’

    In 2002, Daniel wrestled in Martial Art Wrestling arranged in Japan, where he becomes the ring of honor Champion till 2006. During 2005, he tried his luck in the ROH world championship, where he faces some physical destruction, and that kept him away from the Wrestling for the little time. Daniel came back to the ROH in 2007, and his first match of wrestling was telecast on national television. In 2009, Daniel re-joined WWF again. In his first match with WWE, he defeated Chavo Guerrero and made a debut to NXT in 2010.

    In 2011, He won the World Heavyweight Championship first time. He appeared in TV reality series in 2013. During 2014, Daniel again got a break from the career due to some injury, and later in 2015, he decided to participate in the match of Royal Rumble. Daniel got retired from the professional wrestling in February 2016, and after that, he named General Manager of the SmackDown. Currently, he is away from the ring and living happily with the family.
