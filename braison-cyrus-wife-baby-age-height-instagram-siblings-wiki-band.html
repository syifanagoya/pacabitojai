    Braison Cyrus band

    One of Braison’s recent singles is “Black Water,” a profoundly Southern folk-rock tune that features country music iconic Steve Earle.

    Just one listen to the track, which Braison wrote himself, and there is no denying that it would have fit seamlessly on a Levon Helm album.

    The song was initially inspired by stories coming out of Nashville during a record-breaking heatwave of 2017.

    Braison Cyrus recalled:

    “Me and my drummer were reading stories about people getting out of their cars on the freeway in Nashville, on the 65 and beating the crap out of each other.”

    “So we started from there, and then this murder-y backwoods kind of story started taking shape, and then I just took it from there and ran with it.”

    Regarding the comparisons to The Band’s Helm, Braison Cyrus said:

    “Levon was proud, and he loved putting Southern imagery into his music.”

    “I feel like sometimes the South gets left out of a lot of these spookier stories, unless it has a Deliverance vibe, which I particularly don’t enjoy.”
